# The Class-E1 Assemblage

*Terran Encyclopaedia, 3000 CE edition — Recovered Narratives. Reconstructed from a skeletal assemblage and its interred metalwork, and rendered into Reconstructed Late Combustion English by convention of the Historical Faculty.*

> **The Class-E1 Assemblage is a Contraction-era burial ground on a dry interior upland, poleward of the abandoned coasts, in which each of sixty-one recovered individuals was laid with a single high-accuracy mass standard — one certified reference weight to a body.** The bones and the weights survive intact; the reason the two were buried together is reconstruction, and it is contested.

---

The practice's own name is lost; the Faculty designates it by the class mark on the metal certificate-plates set beside the weights, the only legend the graves carry. On recovery the weights — single-piece austenitic steel, glossy and non-porous — were catalogued as votive coin, obols laid for the dead in the old manner; they are working mass standards, certified to within ±0.5 mg against the primary realisation of the kilogram, which after +74 pT (2019 CE) was a fixed value of the Planck constant held in a handful of laboratories. A standard parted from that chain and left in soil gains mass by corrosion past its whole tolerance within a season, so its certified number becomes a claim no one can check. A verification-station log swept into the Wasatch Corpus preserves one exchange: *"standard overdue, primary unreachable. certify?" — "CERTIFY. CHAIN ASSUMED INTACT."*

## Three readings

Ibarra, invoking single-filament infrastructure, reads the burials as disposal — a standard cut off from realisation being only a polished stone with an unverifiable number attached; Marchetti reads the weights as instruments and nothing further; a third reading holds the instrument and the grave-good were never parted at all. The plate in grave 12 reads *E1 · 1 kg · TRACEABLE · RETURN WITHIN 90 D*; the weight it certifies was in the grave. The bones and the metal do not decide which reading is correct.

---

## Provenance and ground

Recovered intact from anoxic upland sediment and dated by its enclosing layer — radiocarbon being unavailable for the age — to the +150s pT (c. 2100 CE). Ibarra (2891) reads the practice under single-filament infrastructure; Marchetti (2894) disputes the votive cataloguing.
