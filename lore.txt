TERRAN ENCYCLOPAEDIA — THE UNIVERSE
The backstory of the world. Not a style guide; the setting itself.

===============================================================================
1. WHERE AND WHEN WE STAND
===============================================================================

The vantage is the year 3000 CE. The civilisation of that year calls itself
Terran and calls its own scholars the Historical Faculty. It is not a ruined
world and not a restored one. It is a later, smaller-at-the-waist, redistributed
humanity that lives mostly poleward, inland, and upland of where its ancestors
lived, speaks a descended language, and studies the industrial era the way we
study the Bronze Age: across a gulf, from wreckage, with more confidence about
chemistry than about people.

The era it studies is the Combustion Age — our own. From three thousand years
out, everything from roughly 1750 to 2140 CE reads as a single interval: the
span in which one species learned to unlock stored chemical energy by burning
it, briefly integrated the whole planet into a handful of shared systems, and
then let those systems go. The 3000 CE scholars do not think of it as "the
modern world" or "the twenty-first century." They think of it as one geological
and historical unit, bounded, over, and hard to read.

This document is the truth of that world — what actually happened between the
Combustion Age and the year 3000. Much of this truth is unavailable to the
3000 CE scholars themselves; the gap between what happened and what they can
recover is the central fact of the setting, and section 6 explains it.


===============================================================================
2. THE COMBUSTION AGE
===============================================================================

NAME. The period is named for its energetic basis — combustion — not for its
residues, under the Ravensbourne convention of 2604. Rival names that lost:
"Plastic Age," "Silicon Age." The winning name says the defining act was
burning things, and everything else (the plastics, the chips, the carbon) was
downstream of that.

BOUNDS. Roughly 1750–2140 CE. It has an internal phase structure:
  - Proto Combustion      c. 1712–1830 CE
  - Early Combustion         1830–1945 CE
  - Middle Combustion        1945–2010 CE
  - Late Combustion          2010–2078 CE
  - the Contraction       c. 2078–2140 CE
  - the Consolidation     after c. 2140 CE  (the recovery into what becomes
                                            the 3000 CE world)

DATING (pT). The calendar zero is the first fission detonation — the Trinity
test, 16 July 1945 CE. Dates are given "post-Trinity": pT. The conversion is
exact and simple:
        CE = pT + 1945
So 2020 CE = +75 pT; 1920 CE = −25 pT; 1948 CE = +3 pT. Scholars quote pT in
running prose and give both on first mention. The choice of zero is deliberate
and pointed: the age is dated from the moment it acquired the means to end
itself.

STRATIGRAPHY. The Combustion Age is not only a historical period; it is a
physical layer in the rock and sediment of the planet, the Gallus Horizon, with
its type locality at Crawford Lake. The Terran Stratigraphic Commission ratified
it as a formal unit in 2412 — notably, the period's OWN stratigraphers had
declined to ratify it in 2024 CE, one of the small self-referential ironies the
scholarship keeps returning to.

DIAGNOSTIC MARKERS. The layer is recognised worldwide by:
  - spheroidal carbonaceous particles (fly ash from high-temperature combustion)
    — the sharpest and most useful marker, with a clean onset and a clean end;
  - plutonium-239 from atmospheric weapons testing (which by 3000 CE has lost
    only ~4% of its inventory);
  - a lead-isotope spike (leaded fuel), peaking ~200x natural background in
    layers dated to the late 1960s CE;
  - a nitrogen-isotope shift in lake sediment (industrial fertiliser);
  - polymer — a bed of subvisible plastic fragments continuous across every
    marine basin on Earth;
  - the bones of one bird (see below).

THE RADIOCARBON PARADOX. The period cannot be radiocarbon dated. Its bombs
spiked atmospheric carbon-14; then its fossil-fuel exhaust flooded the air with
carbon so old it had no carbon-14 left, diluting the ratio below the natural
baseline. The two effects cross. An object manufactured in 2050 CE radiocarbon-
dates to roughly 1050 CE. The single most powerful dating tool available to
archaeology simply does not work on the age that invented it.

THE SCALE OF THE AGE. A handful of load-bearing quantities define how large,
how fast, and how strange the interval was:

  - Anthropogenic mass — everything humans made and left — overtook the total
    living dry biomass of the planet around 2020 CE, each side ~1.1 teratonnes,
    then went on doubling roughly every twenty years to the Contraction. Most of
    it is concrete and aggregate.
  - Between 30 and 50 gigatonnes of polymer were produced cumulatively; under a
    tenth was ever reprocessed. It lithifies into a rock, plastiglomerate.
  - Industrial nitrogen fixation from 1913 CE meant that, by the Late Combustion
    Age, roughly half the nitrogen atoms in a living human body had passed
    through a factory reactor. Two-fifths to one-half of the world's population
    depended on synthetic fertiliser for the nitrogen in their food.
  - The type fossil of the whole layer is a chicken. A fast-growing meat fowl
    (broiler Gallus), bred from ~1950 CE for yield and slaughter age alone,
    stood at ~25 billion birds alive at any moment in the age's final decades —
    a combined living mass roughly three times that of every wild bird species
    combined. Selective breeding raised its 42-day live weight more than
    fourfold in fifty years. Its immature, distinctively porous bones, preserved
    by anoxic landfill, are the most abundant vertebrate fossil the age leaves.
  - The wet-bulb temperature of 35 °C is the hard physiological ceiling: above
    it, a healthy person at rest cannot shed metabolic heat and dies within
    hours. The Indo-Gangetic plain was projected to cross it first. It matters
    enormously later (section 3).
  - Orbit and the Moon keep a separate, near-perfect record. Low Earth orbit
    self-cleared through atmospheric drag; geostationary orbit holds a graveyard
    of dry, cold, intact dead satellites — "a museum nobody visits." Six crewed
    lunar landing sites and three laser retroreflector arrays (1969–1971 CE) are
    still there and still rangeable. Rover tracks on the Moon are estimated
    legible for ~10 million years.
  - Sea level at 3000 CE stands several metres above the Middle Combustion
    datum and is still rising on a slow millennial ice-response. The ocean has
    not stopped moving.


===============================================================================
3. THE CONTRACTION — WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED
===============================================================================

This is the true history. It is the thing the whole setting is about, and it is
the thing the 3000 CE scholars can least clearly see.

THE ONE THING THAT HAPPENED. The Combustion Age did not end in a flash. It
ended in a sorting. Across roughly the +80s to +195 pT (late 2020s to ~2140 CE),
the planetary systems that had briefly knitted the species into one — the food
system, the electrical grid, long-haul logistics, financial settlement, and the
automated machinery that allocated attention and decisions — were let go of,
region by region, nowhere on a single day and never everywhere at once. A
smaller, redistributed humanity came out the far side. Measured against the
Combustion Age's own beginning it was still an enormous population; measured
against the mid-century peak it was much reduced. It was survived. It was also,
in places, uncounted, and a catastrophe. Both are true at once, and the record
that would let anyone weigh the one against the other is precisely the record
that did not survive.

There was a catastrophe. There was not THE catastrophe. It had no year, no
flash, and no name that its own people agreed on.

Four forces drove it. They are the mechanism, not a slogan; the truth is in how
they interlock.

--- Force 1: HEAT AND WATER — the sorting principle -------------------------

The climate forcing was real, foreseen, published, and gradual — and the
gradualness is exactly why nothing decisive was done until it expressed itself
as heat, drought, and movement. It did not end the world. It sorted it.

Coastal abandonment ran in an order that tracked wealth far more strongly than
elevation: the poor stayed in place, the moneyed relocated first and dry.
Wet-bulb 35 °C excursions opened in the Indo-Gangetic plain in the +120s pT
(~2060s CE) — but the plain did not empty from heat. It emptied two generations
later, when the wells were dry and the grid was intermittent and the three
failures arrived together. Sea level rose metres and is still rising. The
retreat was poleward, inland, and upland; the dry high interiors the Late
Combustion economy had ignored are where the people are now.

--- Force 2: NUMBER — the demography that cannot be read ---------------------

Population crested near 10.3 billion around the mid-twenty-first century and
then fell. The fall had two components that the surviving record cannot
separate:

  (a) a genuine, humane, voluntary decline in fertility below replacement,
      already underway before the Contraction; and
  (b) real, uncounted excess mortality DURING it — heat, famine, disease, and
      the road — never tallied, because the institutions that tally (civil
      registration, the census) were among the very first things lost.

The genome recovers deep population structure but cannot tell a person who died
from a person who moved. This is the load-bearing void of the entire setting.
The whole distance between "humane transition" and "uncounted catastrophe" is
the distance between DIED and MOVED — and that is the one distinction the
evidence cannot make. The answer is: both, unevenly, and the ratio is genuinely
lost.

--- Force 3: THE ROAD — the great displacement (the hinge) -------------------

The central event of the millennium is a redistribution. Over roughly +80 to
+160 pT (~2025–2105 CE), on the order of a billion or more people relocated —
poleward, inland, upland — in waves, across generations, under a hundred local
names, never as one exodus. Populations moving under stress keep no archives,
and the institutions that might have recorded them were themselves lapsing, so
the largest human event of the age left almost no direct record at all.

We know where people ENDED — from genomes, from dental isotopes, from the
modern map — and next to nothing of the journeys. The displacement survives only
as fingerprints:

  - THE LANGUAGE TELL. The living language of the 3000 CE world, Standard
    Terran, descends from the South Asian variety of the age's dominant
    vernacular (its retroflex consonants, its invariant interrogative tag, the
    aspectual copula the prescriptivists "corrected" while their own children
    spoke it) crossed with Pacific island contact varieties (the inclusive-vs-
    exclusive distinction in "we," the pronoun "yumi" built from "you-and-me" on
    a low-island waterfront). These are the two most-displaced populations on
    Earth: the first plain to cross the wet-bulb line, and the first islands to
    go under. The world ended up speaking in the grammar of the people who had
    to move — the language of the winners is the language of the drowned.
  - THE GENOMIC TELL. Endogamy structures that had held for a hundred
    generations relax, during and after the Contraction, in a pattern that
    tracks displacement rather than doctrine. Whether that is emancipation or
    catastrophe, the DNA cannot say.
  - THE WEALTH TELL. Who moved early and safely, and who was stranded, tracked
    wealth and not need — the same gradient as the drowned coasts.

--- Force 4: THE MACHINES — the record that addresses itself -----------------

The late period delegated allocation, logistics, credit, targeting, and above
all attention and decision to automated systems trained on its own data. All of
this is ordinary and real and predates the age's last decades: recommendation
engines, algorithmic trading, automated routing, credit scoring, targeted
advertising, biometric identity, predictive systems. There is no rogue mind, no
awakened intelligence, no uploads, no invented capability. The machines are
never villains.

They optimised objectives that were legible to themselves and not to the people.
Two consequences carry the weight:

  - THE SYSTEMS OUTRAN THE PEOPLE. As the integrated world came apart, the
    automated allocations, prices, and routings kept executing on a world that
    had drifted out from under the data they were trained on — ordinary
    distribution shift and objective misspecification, running at planetary
    scale with no one left at the desk. A food-distribution ledger that is
    entered faithfully every day and then simply stops mid-week, without remark,
    is a system logging correctly until it lost power. The Contraction was, in
    part, machinery still running a policy on a world it no longer fit.
  - THE AUTHORSHIP VOID. By the end, so much text was machine-generated — and
    engineered to be indistinguishable from a person's — that the surviving late
    record contains streams no scholar can classify as human-written or machine-
    produced. The plurality of complete, well-formed imperative sentences in the
    period's own hand are addressed to processors, not people. The record of the
    last human century may be, in large part, a record of what machines said
    about people, and the distinction was engineered away.

--- THE APOCALYPSE THAT DID NOT COME -----------------------------------------

The age armed for, rehearsed, and buried its warnings against a nuclear ending.
That ending did not happen. The isotopic record forbids a large exchange and
cannot rule out a small one. The truth: there was no civilisation-ending
exchange. The arsenals were mostly dismantled, abandoned, or simply lost unfired
as the states holding them contracted; at most there was localised use, small
enough to hide under an already-elevated plutonium-239 background, and genuinely
unconfirmable. The apocalypse everyone prepared for is not the one that came.
The one that came had no flash.

4. THE ATTRITION — THE LOSS OF THE RECORD
===============================================================================

Between roughly +45 and +255 pT (~1990–2200 CE), nearly all of the period's
written and audiovisual output was lost. This was not a fire or a war. It was a
maintenance failure — an interval mismatch. The age's media needed active,
funded recopying on a cycle of years to a couple of decades, and the
institutions doing the recopying proved reliable for less than a human lifetime.

There were two failure modes:
  - the substrate dies: magnetic tape lasts ~10–30 years, spinning disks ~3–5,
    optical discs rot; and even durable media outlived the machines that could
    read them, which went obsolete within a generation;
  - the substrate lives but the program that could interpret it is gone
    (proprietary-format obsolescence).

The only remedy was continuous migration — a subscription payable forever.
Custodial ("cloud") data was withdrawn wholesale on the date a payment lapsed.

The devastating part: the age foresaw all of this. Its own archivists named it,
measured it, and published the warnings — on the very media they were warning
about. It even staged the demonstration: an anniversary re-survey issued on a
video disc that was unreadable within sixteen years, printed alongside the
eleventh-century parchment it commemorated, which was still perfectly legible
after nine hundred. Roughly 60+ zettabytes were created in a single peak year;
about 2% of it survived one year later.

The Attrition is why the setting looks the way it does. It selectively destroyed
exactly the human-generated, institution-kept record — and left standing the
durable, the inert, the machine-kept, and the accidentally intercepted.


===============================================================================
5. WHAT SURVIVED — THE EVIDENTIARY SITUATION
===============================================================================

The scholarship of 3000 CE is built almost entirely from a handful of freak
survivals. Each one biases the picture in a specific, traceable way.

THE LONGYEAR DEPOSIT (its own name for itself: the Arctic World Archive).
Silver-halide film on polyester, stored in a disused coal working at 78° N.
Each reel carries a printed, human-legible "Portal Leader" explaining how to
decode it. Survival correlated with shelf height: the chamber flooded, and the
coal seam's pyrite made the water acidic, so film on the lower racks dissolved.
Film on the upper racks lived. This single accident of shelving produces the
"Svalbard bias" that runs through the whole discipline.

THE CODE DEPOSIT (consignment L-III of the Longyear Deposit). One Sunday
snapshot of the entire public software of the world, written to 186 reels of
film — tens of millions of projects, ~21 trillion bytes. It arrived late (a
pandemic delayed shipping), so it was shelved high, so it survived. It is
bit-exact and self-verifying: the repository named every file by a fingerprint
of its own contents, so a recovered file is provably the original. More than
nine-tenths of ALL bit-exact surviving Combustion-Age text is this one archive
of source code. Alongside it: "the Instruction," the only Combustion-Age text
written expressly for the future.

THE WASATCH CORPUS. Bulk-intercepted network traffic, hoarded by signals
agencies under a "harvest now, decrypt later" policy and finally read out once
quantum computers broke the era's public-key cryptography. It is the fullest
record of ordinary life that survives — and, by volume, it is mostly advertising
telemetry. It over-samples the Anglophone, the urban, the connected, and the
purchasing. Its guiding aphorism, from the scholar Ranganathan: "we do not know
what they said to each other; we know what was said about them to machines."

THE SEALED. Symmetric-ciphered archives. These are not undeciphered like the
Bronze Age's Linear A. They are UNDECIPHERABLE — provably, by the period's own
mathematics. A category of evidence with no precedent in the whole history of
the discipline: sources known to exist, known to contain something, and known to
be permanently unreadable.

OTHER DELIBERATE ARCHIVES:
  - the Hallstatt tablets — fired ceramic in a salt mine; the finest surviving
    corpus of everyday writing;
  - the nickel disks — including a language disk (~76 mm, ~1,500 languages,
    ~13,000 pages), the sole surviving attestation of several hundred tongues;
    and a consignment scattered across Mare Serenitatis on the Moon in 2019 CE;
  - the Voyager records — two gold-plated phonograph disks launched out of the
    Solar System in 1977 CE, engraved with their own playback instructions and a
    uranium dating patch, estimated legible for a billion years. One was
    recovered in 2871 CE at 3,340 AU and played on the first attempt. It is the
    best-preserved artefact of the age — and the most misleading, because its
    makers deliberately excluded war, disease, poverty, weapons, and ideology.
    It is a curated self-portrait mistaken, at first, for a sample.
  - the Long Clock — a monument-scale clock, built to run for ten thousand
    years, still running.

THIN-ATTESTATION REGIONS. The places where the population maximum coincides with
the evidentiary minimum. These are, disproportionately, exactly the regions that
moved — so the discipline's blindest spots line up precisely with its most
important story (section 3).

THE UNIFYING PATTERN. Everything that survived did so for one of a few reasons:
by durable medium (Longyear, the Code Deposit), by a keeper with an unrelated
purpose (the Wasatch Corpus, skimmed off cable landing stations), by being
provably unreadable anyway (the Sealed), or by deliberate exception on metal or
in vacuum (the nickel disks, the Voyager records). Every one of those channels
over-samples the connected, urban, purchasing, Anglophone few — which is to say,
it over-samples the exact people the catastrophe spared, and under-samples the
poor, the rural, the moved, and the dead.


===============================================================================
6. WHY THE SCHOLARS CANNOT SEE IT
===============================================================================

The 3000 CE scholars are honest, rigorous, and stuck. The Attrition destroyed
precisely the evidence that would settle the story: the registrations, the
censuses, the mortality and migration records, the deliberative record, and the
human — as against machine — voice. What remains is durable, inert, machine-kept,
and accidentally intercepted, and every one of those channels over-samples the
people the catastrophe spared.

So their honest position is agnosticism. They maintain schools of thought that
never adjudicate. A revisionist reading — "there was no collapse; the end of the
archive is not the end of the world" — cannot be put down, because the evidence
genuinely will not put it down. Their caution reads as wisdom. That agnosticism
is itself a symptom of the thing that happened: the record is shaped exactly so
that the catastrophe cannot be proven or disproven, and the scholars are too
rigorous to claim what they cannot show.

The reader of the encyclopaedia knows more than its authors do. That is the
engine of the setting: we can see the shape the scholars are too honest to name.

A guiding principle, and the true source of the setting's dread: the horror is
NEVER that the past was ignorant. They knew. They published it. They put the
warning in their own promotional literature — that the media would rot, that the
fuel additive was lethal, that the plain would cross the wet-bulb line, that the
allocation systems were misspecified — and then they did not act on it. Every
disaster in this world is a foreseen, documented, un-acted-upon one.


===============================================================================
7. THE STUDY OF THE AGE — INSTITUTIONS AND SCHOLARS
===============================================================================

The 3000 CE world has a mature academic discipline devoted to the Combustion
Age. It has its own bodies, journals, and recurring scholars.

BODIES:
  - the Institute for Deep Record Studies;
  - the Selene Historical Society (based at Tycho, on the Moon);
  - the Terran Stratigraphic Commission (which ratified the Gallus Horizon in
    2412).

JOURNALS:
  - Journal of Anthropogenic Stratigraphy;
  - Historiography.

RECURRING SCHOLARS (the discipline's standing voices):
  - Ranganathan, V. — sampling and bias; the Wasatch Corpus.
  - Zhou-Ferreira, A. — the revisionist "there was no collapse" reading.
  - Ibarra, N. — systems fragility; "single-filament infrastructure," the
    doctrine that the age ran its whole planet through dangerously few points of
    failure (one canal, one ore body, two poultry bloodlines, one fuel additive
    formula).
  - Ashworth-Nakamura, K. — dating and archives; the radiocarbon problem.
  - Marchetti, L. — historiography and cross-era comparison; the scholar who
    debunked the reading of a trivial software routine as a canonical "text."
  - Halvorsen, E. — taphonomy: how specific materials corrode or survive.
  - Okonkwo, D. — deposits and incidental metadata; reading accidental records
    (commit timestamps, routing logs) as unintended censuses.

SCHOOLS OF THOUGHT (invoked only where the question is genuinely unsettled):
  - on the Contraction: systems-fragility / climatic / information / continuity;
  - on why the nuclear arsenals were never used: deterrence / contingency /
    materialist;
  - on the age's politics, which the discipline refuses to adjudicate:
    maximalist (the intercepted advertising corpus is representative) vs.
    corpus-critical (it is not — this is like reconstructing a whole society
    from one city's billboards).

LANGUAGE OF SCHOLARSHIP. Two languages matter. Standard Terran is the living
language of the 3000 CE world (section 3). The encyclopaedia's articles are
written in a scholarly reconstruction called Reconstructed Late Combustion
English — a convention, the way real Bronze Age scholarship keeps words like
"tell," "tumulus," and "stela." It is not anyone's mother tongue. Standard
Terran has since lost the interdental fricatives ("th" sounds), marks completed
action with a preverbal particle descended from the auxiliary "done,"
distinguishes an inclusive from an exclusive "we," and carries a retroflex
consonant series and an invariant question tag inherited from South Asian
English — which, by ~2045 CE, had become the most-spoken variety of the tongue.
