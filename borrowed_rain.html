Borrowed Rain

Terran Encyclopaedia, 3000 CE edition — Recovered Narratives. Reconstructed from an intercepted genebank telemetry stream and rendered into Reconstructed Late Combustion English by convention of the Historical Faculty.

“Borrowed Rain” is the Historical Faculty’s gloss for the lost vernacular name of pearl-millet accession PM-0442-B, a dryland landrace cold-stored against future need. The name the farmers who kept it used is gone; only its sense survives, in translation.

In 1974 CE (+29 pT), a drylands-crops mission on the Middle Gangetic margin catalogued PM-0442-B, a pearl-millet (Pennisetum glaucum) line pooled from several villages’ separate seed-stocks after the 1965–67 dry years (+20–22 pT) — a genomic trace of who had to trade seed with whom, not of any breeding programme:

1974-11-03 // PM-0442-B // moisture 6.1% // vigor high // farmer states seed traded in from kin displaced by the ‘66 dry.

Storage kinetics

Orthodox seed ages by first-order loss of germinability, its rate set by moisture and temperature; Harrington’s rule holds storage life roughly doubling per 5 °C drop, or per point of moisture shed. Sealed at 6.1% and −18 °C, PM-0442-B’s predicted life ran to centuries — a debt against continuous refrigeration nobody re-budgeted for. Telemetry shows the hold drifting to −4 °C from +134 pT (2079 CE) and not recovering. No further reading was logged.

Provenance and ground

Recovered from the cold store’s own monitoring traffic, swept into the Wasatch Corpus and read out once its cipher fell. Fenwick-Rao (2887) supplies the gloss of the lost name.
