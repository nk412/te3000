# Variety ledger

A map of what every existing entry already does, so that each new one can go somewhere the
corpus has not been. This is the durable mechanism behind the brief's "no formula, ebbs and
flows" rule (see `prompt.md` → VARIETY and PROCESS). It is not rendered into the site.

**How to use it (PROCESS step 0).** Before researching a new entry, read this whole file — not
just the last few rows. Pick a register, a scale, a lead opener, a section architecture, a mood,
and a dominant device such that the *combination* matches no existing row, and lean toward what
the corpus is short of. Write those choices down before you start. After the entry is finished,
append its row (PROCESS step 8).

---

## Already over-used — steer away unless the subject genuinely demands it

- **The two-readings device** (a thing "held by some to be X, by others its opposite"): Voyager, Nuclear, Onkalo, Bornholm. The brief already warns it turns into a reflex. Four in nineteen is the ceiling, not the target.
- **The aggregate-number lead or ending** (landing on one big figure): Catalytic, Intermodal, Leaded, Broiler, Nitrogen. A number is a strong landing and has become the default one. Rotate it out.
- **The "plain topic headers × 4" architecture**: roughly half the corpus. Vary the *count* (2–3, or 6–8) and the *cut* (by phase, by component, by failure mode, by the steps of an inference), or use numbered sections, or lead with a chronology.
- **The "standard, ~1,400–2,000 word" scale**: most entries. The corpus has almost no true short fragments and only a few long features — it needs both extremes to read like a real, unevenly-catalogued archive.
- **Opening on a bold definition**: near-universal, and fine as a house default — but rotate in leads that open on a stat, a bare dated event, or the contested reading.

## Under-used — reach for these

- A genuine **fragment / stub**: a few hundred words on one recovered object, one intercepted line, one surviving record — glossed and stopped. These should become common.
- A **catalogue / list** entry ("known X of the Combustion Age"): a short frame, then a terse annotated list, where the list is the correct form.
- Registers not yet tried: **comic / wry-light**; **promotional prose quoted and dissected**; **litigious / regulatory**; **claim-then-recant** (a period assertion, then its correction).
- Endings that are **not** a number and **not** a quiet flat fact: an open question stated bare; a single dated event; a reconstructed line of period speech.
