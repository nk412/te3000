The Delta Register

Terran Encyclopaedia, 3000 CE edition — Recovered Narratives. Reconstructed from 2,814 nights of intercepted replication traffic in the Wasatch Corpus and rendered into Reconstructed Late Combustion English by convention of the Historical Faculty.

The Delta Register is a Contraction-era registry of persons on the move, known only from the nightly change-traffic of its mirror copy, skimmed at an Arabian Sea cable landing and read out once the era’s public-key cryptography fell. The delta instructions and their literal payloads are record; everything the registry already held, including its own name, is beyond reconstruction.

On the night of 9 June +133 pT (2078 CE), a cable landing station on the Arabian Sea began retaining a stream that recrossed it for 2,814 consecutive nights: replication traffic from a registry of persons, kept current by delta encoding, the period’s standard economy for a link billed by the byte. A delta is an instruction list — copy a span the far end already holds, add these literal new bytes — and the mirror’s rule was correct: data both ends held was never sent again. The baseline snapshot had crossed the cable once, in +130 pT (2075 CE), before retention began, and under the rule it never crossed again. The stream therefore encodes the register only conditional on a baseline that does not survive; every copy points into nothing, and what stays legible, permanently, is the additions.

The additions

New entries and amended status fields, in the South Asian variety of the age’s vernacular whose word for a household relocation, shift, survives in Standard Terran. A representative decoded run:

COPY  65536, 0
ADD   17, "shifted; Barmer-4"
COPY  131072, 65551

The registry’s own name for itself lay inside a copy span and is unrecoverable.

The count

Each night’s frame closed with an uncompressed sequence counter. Okonkwo reads one increment per registrant — the widest count of persons in motion the Contraction’s first decade left anywhere. Ranganathan objects that a registry of the moving counts registrations, not persons: a household re-registering at each waypoint is counted at each waypoint. Zhou-Ferreira reads 2,814 nights of a mirror faithfully kept as evidence of administration, not emergency. The stream cannot decide among them. On its last night, 21 February +141 pT (2086 CE), the counter closed at 11,306,441.

Provenance and ground

The stream survives because a signals agency skimmed the cable under the harvest-now policy and because the era’s public-key wrapping later fell to the quantum decipherment. The delta layer beneath it is not a cipher and required no breaking; it is conditional by construction. Copy–add instruction lists were an open standard of the period, and the property that only the add payloads decode without the source window is provable from the format’s own specification, which survives bit-exact in the Code Deposit.
