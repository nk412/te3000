14 articles on the Combustion Age (c. 1750–2140 CE).

Featured article

The Combustion Age (also Combustion Interval; stratigraphically the Gallus Horizon) is the period of Terran history defined by the systematic oxidation of geologically sequestered carbon as the primary energy basis of human society. It is conventionally dated from c. 1750 CE to c. 2140 CE, though regional chronologies differ by more than a century, and no single terminal date commands consensus.

  • … that the type fossil of the age is a broiler chicken, bred to grow too fast to stand, and the most numerous bird in Terran history?
  • … that a lunar rover’s 101 metres of wheel tracks will outlast every stone structure its makers ever built by a factor of about ten thousand?
  • … that the largest human gathering in history leaves no archaeological trace at all, because the river takes the sand?

Combustion Age

The Terran historical period, c. 1750–2140 CE.

Attrition, the

The Attrition is the near-total loss of the Combustion Age's written and recorded output — not to any catastrophe, but because its dominant media held information only for as long as a funded institution kept copying it forward, on a cycle shorter than a human life.

Bhārat, Combustion Age

The South Asian peninsular polity from +55 pT (2000 CE) to the Contraction.

Bornholm Deposit

The Combustion-Age chemical-munitions dumpsite in the Bornholm Basin of the Baltic Sea, the largest single deposit of its kind by tonnage.

Code Deposit

The Code Deposit is the single surviving snapshot of the Combustion Age's public software: every openly shared program on one large repository, copied in a day and shelved for a thousand years — the most complete text the period bequeathed, and among the least representative.

Combustion-Age Languages

The spoken and written languages of the Combustion Age, and the reasons almost none of them can be heard.

Cryogenic Biobank

A cryogenic biobank held living cells, gametes, or embryos in a glassy, ice-free solid at or below −196 °C, a state that persisted only for as long as someone kept paying to refill the liquid nitrogen.

Industrial Nitrogen Fixation

Industrial nitrogen fixation is the artificial synthesis of ammonia from atmospheric nitrogen and hydrogen gas, the single reaction on which the Late Combustion Age's population, its longest war, and a fair share of its dead coastal water are all, independently, hung.

Intermodal Shipping Container

A corrugated-steel box, built to one of two standard lengths and closed by a single repeated mechanical fitting at each corner, that let cargo cross unopened from ship to railcar to truck chassis and became, within two decades of its introduction, the load-bearing unit of the Combustion Age's seaborne trade.

Leaded Petrol

Leaded petrol is the tetraethyl-lead antiknock fuel additive sold for ninety-nine consecutive years after a fatal occupational poisoning had already demonstrated its danger, and legible now as a single continuous concentration curve in polar ice that no institution built and no institution meant to keep.

Longyear Deposit

The Combustion-Age film archive at 78°N.

Onkalo

The Combustion-Age deep geological repository for spent nuclear fuel beneath Olkiluoto Island, on Finland's west coast.

Svalbard Seed Vault

The Combustion-Age reserve repository of crop seed on the Arctic archipelago of Svalbard, the backup collection of last resort for the period's plant genetic resources.

Voyager Records

The Voyager records are a pair of identical gold-plated audiovisual disks launched out of the Solar System in +32 pT (1977 CE), one of which — retrieved at 3,340 astronomical units in +926 pT (2871 CE) — is the best-preserved artefact of the Combustion Age and the most misleading.
