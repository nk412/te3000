  • Borrowed Rain"Borrowed Rain" is the Historical Faculty's gloss for the lost vernacular name of pearl-millet accession PM-0442-B, a dryland landrace cold-stored against future need.
  • The Low-Light QueueThe Low-Light Queue is the Historical Faculty's gloss for the lost name of a small atoll workforce that, across the +90s to +120s pT (2040s–2060s CE), was routed the images an automated colour-classifier had failed on — and that a founder mutation in its own ancestry made unusually suited to the task.
  • The Long-Wave ReveilleThe Long-Wave Reveille is the Historical Faculty's name for an unidentified longwave time-and-frequency broadcast that, from the Middle Combustion Age into the Contraction, kept a scattered fleet of automated poultry-house lighting controllers in step with a national frequency standard.
  • The Wide SynchronyThe Wide Synchrony is the Historical Faculty's name for a continental alternating-current interconnection — some hundreds of millions of rotating machines held to a single electrical phase — and for the roughly nine minutes in +112 pT (2057 CE) in which that phase came apart across a mid-latitude continental interior.
  • The Narrow HandThe Narrow Hand is the Historical Faculty's gloss for the lost name of a live-captioning workforce — writers who rendered broadcast speech into text in real time, by hand, on a twenty-two-key chorded keyboard, for viewers who could not hear it — and who are now known, through one shared statistical table, to have been unwittingly bound to a distant-water fishing fleet they never touched.
  • The Silent QuarterThe Silent Quarter is the Historical Faculty's gloss for the Grade-of-Service Register of an unnamed rural automatic exchange, and for the one calendar quarter in which the valley trunk network it reported on lost its single connected component.
  • The False StarThe False Star is the Historical Faculty's name for an unidentified optical way-beacon on a Late Combustion Age overland displacement route, recovered only because its flash left a beaded arc of light across one photographer's tracked exposure of the night sky.
  • The Homing ConstructThe Homing Construct is the Historical Faculty's designation for an engineered genetic element, recovered only as its own design source code, built to copy itself onto the matching chromosome and so drive a chosen trait through a wild population faster than ordinary inheritance permits.
  • The Ridge AdvanceThe Ridge Advance is the Historical Faculty's name for a fixed clock-rate offset, undetected in its own period, carried by the highest mast of an unnamed nine-station microwave relay chain built to shorten the light-path between two automated clearing floors.
  • The Last SoundingThe Last Sounding is the name — given by an early Consolidation-era listening community and never superseded — for the final years of automated transmission from a single mooring in a North Atlantic overturning-circulation monitoring array, received in its own time, and some ways since, as an intentional human signal.
  • The Class-E1 AssemblageThe Class-E1 Assemblage is a Contraction-era burial ground on a dry interior upland, poleward of the abandoned coasts, in which each of sixty-one recovered individuals was laid with a single high-accuracy mass standard — one certified reference weight to a body.
  • The Enamel AssayThe Enamel Assay is the Historical Faculty's name for a Late Combustion technique that read the strontium- and oxygen-isotope signature locked into a person's tooth enamel to adjudicate where that person had spent early childhood, deployed at inland relief and residency stations to test the origin claims of people on the move.
  • The Nine of FifteenThe Nine of Fifteen is the Historical Faculty's name for a Late Combustion custodial community, reconstructed from its burial ground, whose members each held one metal-engraved share of a split cryptographic key, any nine of which would have opened one of the Sealed — and which was never again brought to nine.
  • The Morbidity ReturnThe Morbidity Return is the Historical Faculty's gloss for a Middle Combustion district notification office — a small public-health facility to which physicians were required to send weekly counts of reportable contagious disease — known only from one filled specimen form, preserved in an otherwise near-unattested delta vernacular on the nickel language disk.
  • The Crossing LedgerThe Crossing Ledger is a Contraction-era fee book from a landing on a 260-kilometre strait, recording the unnamed carrier who was, for eleven years, the only connection between the data networks of its two shores.
