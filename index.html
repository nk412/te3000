  • Combustion AgeThis article is about the Terran historical period, c. 1750–2140 CE.
  • Bhārat, Combustion AgeThis article covers the South Asian peninsular polity from +55 pT (2000 CE) to the Contraction.
  • Longyear DepositThe Longyear Deposit is the Combustion Age's most deliberate answer to the loss of its own record: silver-halide images on inert polyester, laid up in a spent coal working at 78°N and built to be read a millennium later by light and a lens alone.
  • Combustion-Age LanguagesThis article is about the spoken and written languages of the Combustion Age, and the reasons almost none of them can be heard.
  • Bornholm DepositThe Bornholm Deposit is the largest documented single site of the Combustion Age's sea-dumped chemical weapons: a defeated arsenal let down into the cold Baltic in the belief that deep water would break it down, and instead kept whole by it.
  • OnkaloOnkalo is the most completely excavated of the Combustion Age's deep geological repositories for spent nuclear fuel, and the discipline's standard case of a barrier built to outlast, by orders of magnitude, the institution that was meant to remember what it held.
  • Voyager RecordsThe Voyager Records are the two identical gold-plated phonograph disks the Combustion Age launched out of the Solar System at +32 pT — the best-preserved artefact of the age, and the most misleading.
  • Svalbard Seed VaultThe Svalbard Seed Vault was the Combustion Age's reserve for duplicate crop seed, and the standard example of a deliberate archive that was drawn down and refilled exactly once — in a single full cycle — by a genebank a siege had dispossessed of its home.
  • Industrial Nitrogen FixationIndustrial nitrogen fixation is the catalytic synthesis of ammonia from atmospheric nitrogen and hydrogen, the single reaction that came to supply a large fraction of the Combustion Age's food and, in one season and without a valve moved, the explosives that extended its largest war.
  • Leaded PetrolLeaded petrol was a motor-fuel additive: a lead compound blended into petrol at a few grams per gallon to suppress engine knock, and formulated with two further chemicals whose specified function was to carry the burnt lead out of the engine and into the open air.
  • Attrition, theThe Attrition is the near-total loss of the Combustion Age's written and recorded output — not to any catastrophe, but because its dominant media held information only for as long as a funded institution kept copying it forward, on a cycle shorter than a human life.
  • Code DepositThe Code Deposit is the one bit-exact, self-verifying snapshot the Combustion Age left of its public software — a single Sunday's copy of every openly hosted project, on 186 reels of film — and the fullest surviving text of the age, legible as a record of its makers only in its timestamps.
  • Cryogenic BiobankA cryogenic biobank was a facility that held living cells, gametes, and embryos indefinitely as an ice-free glass suspended in liquid nitrogen at −196 °C — a state kept by no current and no machine, only by the recurring act of refilling the vessel.
  • Intermodal Shipping ContainerThe intermodal shipping container reduced the world's non-bulk sea cargo to one steel box of two lengths and a single corner geometry, and the trade of a planet to two canals — one of which a single grounded ship could, and once did, close for six days.
  • Crash-Survivable Flight RecorderA crash-survivable flight recorder is a sealed instrument, carried aboard powered aircraft from the interval's middle decades and routinely fitted in pairs — a flight-data unit and a cockpit-voice unit — built to preserve the final minutes of flight and cockpit sound through whatever destroyed everything else aboard.
  • Cardiac PacemakerThe cardiac pacemaker is a sealed device, worn inside the chest, that supplies by a timed electrical pulse the heartbeat a failing conduction system can no longer produce; the first patient fitted with one outlived it by more than forty years and some twenty replacements, and died at last of a cause that had nothing to do with his heart.
  • Submarine Telecommunications CableThe submarine telecommunications cable was the seabed conduit that carried the overwhelming share of the Combustion Age's intercontinental traffic, and by its own design held not one bit of it at rest.
  • Broiler GallusThe broiler Gallus is the fast-growing, short-lived domestic fowl engineered through the Combustion Age's final century for meat yield and market age alone, and the type fossil by which the age's own geological horizon is now dated.
  • Mass Coral BleachingMass coral bleaching is the thermally driven expulsion, by a reef-building coral, of the symbiotic algae that supply it most of its energy and all of its colour, leaving the bare white carbonate skeleton visible through clear, starving tissue.
  • Solar-Powered Planetary RoverThe Solar-Powered Planetary Rover is a wheeled robotic explorer that drew its power from sunlight rather than from a sealed radioisotope source, and the standing example of a machine remembered across a thousand years for a first-person sentence that a person, not the machine, composed to explain it.
  • Nuclear Weapon AccidentNo sealed nuclear weapon is recorded as having detonated by accident anywhere in the surviving literature; the one Combustion-Age recovery that came closest to a full accounting of a dispersed weapon balanced its inventory to ninety percent and could not close it by a single uranium cylinder.
  • Catalytic ConverterThe catalytic converter was a passive exhaust device that destroyed three regulated pollutants at once on no power but engine waste heat, using a film of three of the rarest metals then mined — and in doing so made a parked car briefly a richer source of those metals than either ore body that produced them.
  • The Kharun CorridorThe Kharun Corridor is the most completely reconstructed of the Combustion Age's automated grain-allocation nodes, and the standard example of a distribution system that went on allocating correctly after the population it served had begun to leave.
