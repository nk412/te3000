# The Silent Quarter

*Terran Encyclopaedia, 3000 CE edition — Recovered Narratives. Reconstructed from a slice of intercepted trunk-alarm telemetry in the Wasatch Corpus and rendered into Reconstructed Late Combustion English by convention of the Historical Faculty.*

> **The Silent Quarter is the Historical Faculty's gloss for the Grade-of-Service Register of an unnamed rural automatic exchange, and for the one calendar quarter in which the valley trunk network it reported on lost its single connected component.** The exchange's own designation, and the valley's, are not recoverable; only its supervisory alarms are, skimmed from a leased data circuit and read out once the era's public-key cryptography fell.

---

It is not, whatever three centuries of citation have made of it, a count of the missing. A Grade-of-Service Register logs circuit occupancy against an engineered target, not a population, and this one — recovered whole, for fourteen rural exchanges strung up a single valley, each trunk-linked so that no two routes to the parent office shared a span — was built to the period's ordinary standard of one call attempt in a hundred meeting every circuit busy, set by Erlang's loss formula. The last surviving alarm frame reads:

> `EXCH 09 — LAST ANS 03:47 — NO FAULT RAISED — NO FURTHER CONTACT`

## Below one connection per node

Rural subscriber counts fell for three decades before any trunk failed outright, and the network's mean degree fell with them, unremarked, from just over two connections per exchange toward one. A random network keeps a single giant connected component only while its mean degree exceeds one; below that line it does not thin, it comes apart, sharply and nearly all at once, exactly as the Erdős–Rényi percolation result says it must. The threshold was crossed in one quarter of +58 pT (2003 CE); four exchanges dropped from the parent office within eleven weeks. The falling occupancy figures were read, for decades afterward, as a census of the valley's remaining residents — an engineered blocking probability mistaken for a headcount, the one arithmetic error the register's own numbers cannot support and never stopped being cited.

---

## Provenance and ground

The register survives complete for the network's last eleven years; the exchanges' names, staffing, and the valley's own name do not. Okonkwo's method first read the occupancy series as an incidental census; Marchetti's objection is the one on record against it.
